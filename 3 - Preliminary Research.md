Preliminary research serves as our intellectual compass, allowing us to explore promising directions before committing substantial resources to full academic studies. Working primarily with University of Virginia students, these initial investigations help us identify viable research questions, test methodologies, and assess potential impact. Similar to white papers in industry, our preliminary research documents emerging insights and foundational discoveries that may later evolve into comprehensive published works. This exploratory approach enables us to remain nimble, pursue innovative paths, and cultivate student researchers while efficiently allocating our scholarly resources toward the most promising avenues of inquiry.

### Food Insecurity & SNAP Access Across the United States
* In partnership with SEED Consulting at the University of Virginia (Fall 2025), this project examines food insecurity and SNAP access through five diversified case studies — Atlanta, GA; Chicago, IL; Kansas City, MO; Twin Falls, ID; and Wolfe County, KY. Across urban, suburban, and rural settings, the research finds that food insecurity is driven less by absolute food shortages than by structural barriers: transportation gaps and car dependency, the uneven distribution of SNAP-accepting retailers that carry fresh produce, restrictive state-level eligibility policies, and benefit levels that fail to keep pace with rising housing and grocery costs. The work highlights the outsized economic role of SNAP (every $1 in benefits generates roughly $1.54 in GDP) and the strain on regional food banks as benefits are cut. These insights informed FinMango's expansion into food security through a location-tailored Food Insecurity / SNAP eligibility calculator and "Mango Stories" — interactive, story-based modules on eating healthy on a budget, accessing food assistance, and understanding the causes of food insecurity.
* **Authors (SEED Consulting, UVA):** Jaiden Khemani (Project Leader), Henna Fernandez, Alex Jennings, Mae Bledsoe, and Matt Tam.
