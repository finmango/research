# Paper 2. Publication and collaboration

## Where this goes

- A working paper first (arXiv, SSRN, or an NBER series). Citable right away, and
  it fits how we work, open and in public.
- Then an applied economics or policy outlet, if the labor-market framing
  carries.
- A short letter format, if the result turns out clean and tightly scoped.

We let the result pick the home. A null result and a strong positive result
don't belong in the same place.

## Collaboration

The econometrics is real, and so is the scrutiny that comes with anything
touching causation, so a co-author who knows this terrain raises both the quality
and the odds. We'd welcome collaborators in housing and financial health,
development economics, and labor economics, especially anyone close to
search-based nowcasting or AI-exposure measurement. Same co-author as Paper 1.

## Before we submit

- [ ] The pre-registration link is in the paper.
- [ ] The replication package is archived with a DOI.
- [ ] The limitations section is honest about causation and saturation.
- [ ] The co-author has signed off.
- [ ] Funding and conflicts disclosed, per the brief.
