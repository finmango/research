# Paper 1. Contribution

The analog of a claim, for a dataset paper. What we release, and what we do and
don't say about it.

## What we release

> We release an open, monthly, state-by-occupation measure of AI-exposed
> household financial stress, built from public exposure indices and actual
> Google Health Trends probabilities, with a documented, reproducible pipeline.

## What it claims

- The signal is well-built, well-documented, and reusable.
- It is consistent with known layoff events, descriptively.
- The divergence-from-baseline normalization handles search saturation.

## What it does not claim

- It does not predict unemployment. That is Paper 2.
- It says nothing causal. It never claims AI caused any household's distress.

## Why it stands alone

The dataset is a contribution in its own right. There is no open, high-frequency,
state-by-occupation read on AI-exposed financial stress today. Releasing it
openly, with the pipeline, is the contribution. The predictive test comes next,
in Paper 2, and stands on this.
