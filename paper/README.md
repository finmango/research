# Paper Project: AI Exposure and Household Financial Stress

This folder is where we turn the positioning brief
(`../AI_ECONOMIC_IMPACT_2026.md`) into a paper we can publish.

**Working title:** Do AI-Exposed Workers Show Financial Stress First? A State
by Occupation Leading-Indicator Approach.

**The one sentence the whole paper has to defend:**

> A search-behavior plus AI-exposure signal leads realized occupational
> unemployment by one to three months in high-exposure occupations across US
> states.

That's it. Everything in here serves that sentence.

## How this works

Each numbered folder is one step. Do them roughly in order. There's one rule
you can't break: we don't write the paper (06) until we have the result (05).

Steps 1 to 3 are desk work. We can start them today, no data needed. Steps 4
and 5 are the real work, and everything else waits on them.

## Four things we don't get to forget

These come straight from section 8 of the brief. They're the ones that sink
papers like this.

1. This is measurement, not causation. We never claim AI caused any one
   household's distress. We measure, we lead the data, that's the claim.
2. Search saturation is the first thing a reviewer attacks. So we show the
   divergence-from-baseline approach actually working. We don't just assert it.
3. Augmentation is not substitution. Keep them apart everywhere. Mixing them up
   is the classic way to get this wrong.
4. Pre-register before we see the outcomes. That one move is what makes the
   result believable later.
